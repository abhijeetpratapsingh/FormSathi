# How to Make a Signature Under 10KB for Online Forms

![FormSathi Home overview](../assets/screenshots/home-overview.png)

Many exam, scholarship, and government portals reject signature uploads because the image is too large, too blurry, or cropped badly. If the requirement says `signature under 10KB`, the safest approach is to prepare a clean signature first and then reduce file size carefully.

## When this requirement appears

You will usually see this on:

- government recruitment forms
- scholarship portals
- college admission forms
- exam registration pages

Some portals ask only for a size limit. Others also require exact dimensions, JPG format, or a white background.

## Best way to prepare the signature

1. Sign on plain white paper using dark blue or black ink.
2. Take a clear photo in good light.
3. Crop tightly around the signature, but leave a little white space.
4. Resize before compressing if the portal gives width and height rules.
5. Check the final file size before upload.

## Common mistakes

- compressing before cropping
- keeping too much blank white space around the signature
- uploading PNG when the portal expects JPG
- making the signature so small that the strokes break
- reusing a file made for another portal

## What if 10KB is too hard?

Some signatures cannot reach `10KB` cleanly if the original photo is dark, noisy, or taken from far away. In that case:

- recapture in better light
- crop tighter
- reduce dimensions if allowed
- export again with a lower target

## FAQ

### Is under 10KB required everywhere?

No. It is common, but not universal. Always verify the latest portal instructions.

### Should the signature be JPG or PNG?

Most portals prefer JPG or JPEG, but some accept PNG. Use the format required on the official form page.

### Can I reuse one signature everywhere?

Only if the portal requirements match. Size, dimensions, and background rules often change.
