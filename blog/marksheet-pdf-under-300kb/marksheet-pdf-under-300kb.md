# How to Make a Marksheet PDF Under 300KB

![FormSathi Tools workflows](../assets/screenshots/tools-workflows.png)

`Marksheet pdf under 300kb` is a high-value keyword because education and job portals often need a readable PDF within a strict size limit. Users are not looking for general PDF advice. They want a marksheet that uploads successfully without making the text unreadable.

## What usually needs attention

Marksheet uploads fail when:

- scans are too large
- images are too dark
- the PDF contains too much empty space
- the text becomes blurry after compression

## Best workflow for marksheets

1. Capture each page clearly and evenly.
2. Crop only the useful page area.
3. Arrange pages in order.
4. Convert to PDF.
5. Compress only as much as needed to stay under `300KB`.

## Why readability matters more than the number

Users often focus only on the final size, but for marksheets the text must remain readable. A `280KB` PDF that hides grades is worse than a slightly larger file that the user can still fix before upload.

## FAQ

### Is 300KB a common limit?

Yes, but not universal. Some forms ask for higher or lower limits.

### Should I scan in color or grayscale?

Use whatever keeps the text clearest while staying within the required size.

### What if the PDF goes below 300KB but looks blurry?

Use a cleaner source image, crop tighter, and compress less aggressively.
